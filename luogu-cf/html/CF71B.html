Progress Bar

题目描述

A progress bar is an element of graphical interface that displays the progress of a process for this very moment before it is completed. Let's take a look at the following form of such a bar. A bar is represented as $ n $ squares, located in line. To add clarity, let's number them with positive integers from $ 1 $ to $ n $ from the left to the right. Each square has saturation ( $ a_{i} $ for the $ i $ -th square), which is measured by an integer from $ 0 $ to $ k $ . When the bar for some $ i $ ( $ 1<=i<=n $ ) is displayed, squares $ 1,2,...\ ,i-1 $ has the saturation $ k $ , squares $ i+1,i+2,...\ ,n $ has the saturation $ 0 $ , and the saturation of the square $ i $ can have any value from $ 0 $ to $ k $ . So some first squares of the progress bar always have the saturation $ k $ . Some last squares always have the saturation $ 0 $ . And there is no more than one square that has the saturation different from $ 0 $ and $ k $ . The degree of the process's completion is measured in percents. Let the process be $ t $ % completed. 输入输出格式

输入格式


We are given 3 space-separated integers $ n $ , $ k $ , $ t $ ( $ 1<=n,k<=100 $ , $ 0<=t<=100 $ ).

输出格式


Print $ n $ numbers. The $ i $ -th of them should be equal to $ a_{i} $ .

输入输出样例

输入样例 #1

10 10 54

输出样例 #1

10 10 10 10 10 4 0 0 0 0

输入样例 #2

11 13 37

输出样例 #2

13 13 13 13 0 0 0 0 0 0 0
